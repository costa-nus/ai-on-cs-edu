What CRA states about master's VERBATIM ONLY

Per [S4 summary]:

"Master's degree production ballooned in 2022—23, more than double the prior year — the largest reported master's production in survey history."

Per [S5 summary]:

"A decline in master's and bachelor's degree production... continued growth in total enrollment — especially among new students."

The 2022—23 master's spike is timing-confounded with both COVID rebound and GPT — separating those signals requires master's enrollment numbers I did not extract.

What can be honestly said about CS education and the GPT moment FROM THE VERIFIED DATA

Bachelor's enrollment. Grew steadily from 150,331 to 182,973 across AY 2019—20 to 2022—23 — a 21.7% rise over four years. CRA notes this is "the 16th consecutive year of growth" [S4 summary]. The slope before and after the GPT moment is similar; ChatGPT did not bend the trend visibly at the bachelor's level.

PhD enrollment. Total enrollment grew 8.2% across the same window. The clearest signal is in new PhD enrollment: 3,041 in AY 2022—23 → 3,438 in AY 2023—24 — a +13.1% jump, the steepest single-year rise in this dataset [S4 Table 1]. Applications growth supports this: AY 2023—24 saw 36,361 PhD applications across 95 reporting departments [S4 Table D14], a record.

AI/ML specialty. Share of new PhDs whose specialty is AI/ML rose from 25.2% (2021—22) to 28.4% (2022—23) [S3, S4 Table D4]. This was already the dominant specialty pre-GPT; the post-GPT shift is incremental, not transformational — at least in the specialty mix that's been measured so far. The 2024 report [S5] notes AI/ML "continues to be by far the most popular area of specialty of new PhDs."
