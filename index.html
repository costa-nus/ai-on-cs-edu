May 1, 2026 · U.S. CS Enrollment · Post-ChatGPT Tracker

How did the ChatGPT moment change U.S. computer science education?

Three academic years after ChatGPT's November 2022 debut, the verified national data show two clear shifts: U.S. bachelor's CS enrollment has climbed every year, and the PhD pipeline just posted its first sharp post-GPT jump. A third — the master's market — cannot be measured at the same standard, because no national CS-field enrollment series exists. We flag that gap rather than fill it with a different metric.

▌ The numbers at a glance
  • Bachelor's total enrollment up 22% from AY 2019—20 to AY 2022—23 (150,331 → 182,973). Source: CRA Taulbee Survey, Table 1, ~144 U.S. CS-PhD-granting departments [S1–S4].
  • New bachelor's majors up 37% over the same window (32,180 → 43,993) — the wave has not yet fully reached upper-class enrollment. Source: Taulbee Table 1 [S1–S4].
  • New PhD students +13.1% year-over-year in AY 2023—24 (3,041 → 3,438), the first full academic year that began after ChatGPT — the cleanest "GPT-era" signal in the verified data. Source: Taulbee Survey 2024 [S5].
  • Master's: a coverage gap. CRA Taulbee does not break out master's enrollment, and no other national series tracks U.S. CS-field master's enrollment at a comparable standard. We omit the master's series rather than substitute a different metric.
▌ Methodology — one source, two metrics

All values come from the CRA Taulbee Survey, Table 1. Scope: U.S. CS-PhD-granting departments only (~144 reporting institutions). The Survey distinguishes total Fall enrollment (a stock — who is currently in the program) from new students that year (a flow — who entered). The two are kept on separate panels and never combined.

What we deliberately do not show · CRA Taulbee does not publish master's enrollment, and no other open national source tracks CS-field master's enrollment at a comparable standard. Rather than substitute a different metric (e.g. degrees conferred from a broader institutional scope), the master's line is omitted. Better an honest gap.

Bachelor's · up 22% total, up 37% at the front doorA wave still building

Total bachelor's CS enrollment grew steadily through the GPT moment, ending AY 2022—23 at +22% above AY 2019—20 (150,331 → 182,973 students). New bachelor's majors grew faster — +37% — meaning the surge has not yet fully washed through to upper-division enrollment. Expect total headcount to keep climbing on lag. Source: CRA Taulbee Survey, Table 1 [S1–S4].

PhD · +13.1% year-over-year in AY 2023—24The first clear post-ChatGPT signal

PhD admissions dipped in AY 2020—21 (the COVID effect: index 85.4) and recovered slowly. Then AY 2023—24 — the first full academic year whose admissions cycle ran entirely after ChatGPT's release — produced a +13.1% year-over-year jump in new PhD students (3,041 → 3,438), lifting the index back above its pre-pandemic baseline. This is the cleanest GPT-era signal in the verified national data. Source: CRA Taulbee 2024 [S5].

Master's · a coverage gap, not a flat lineWhat we cannot say from verified data

CRA Taulbee does not break out master's enrollment, and no other open national source tracks U.S. CS-field master's enrollment head-to-head with Taulbee's BS and PhD counts. Rather than substitute a different metric — degrees conferred, on a broader institutional scope — we leave the line out. The post-ChatGPT shift in master's enrollment is real but, as of this update, not measurable from public sources at the same standard as the panels above.

Per-University View · Total Enrollment · Indexed

What does the shift look like at a single school?

Same idea as the national figures, but at one school at a time. Each value below was downloaded by scripts/fetch_university_data.py from the linked university institutional-research source — no third-party aggregators. Click any value's [src] to open the document it came from.

The y-axis shows an index: the school's earliest available year is set to 100, and every later year is scaled relative to that. The underlying measure is total enrollment headcount at the start of each Fall term — i.e. the count of students currently in the program (bachelor's, master's, or PhD), not degrees conferred and not first-time enrollment.

⚠ COVERAGE GAP — READ ME
Per-school enrollment data is hard to find as open primary sources. Most institutional-research offices publish degrees conferred (federally required for IPEDS) but not per-CS-major enrollment in a stable, parseable form. So this view only includes schools whose own IR/department pages publish multi-year CS enrollment headcounts: currently Carnegie Mellon (IRA School-of-Computer-Science PDFs), Georgia Tech (College of Computing fact book), Michigan State (CSE Department page), UW–Madison (DAPIR's public "Trends in Student Enrollments" Tableau viz, Crosstab-extracted), and UMass Amherst (UAIR factbook "Students by Major" PDFs). The following schools were checked and excluded because their public sources do not publish enrollment in a usable form: Stanford, U Washington, U Michigan, UIUC, UMD, UC Berkeley, Purdue, UCSD, UC Irvine, Penn State, Princeton, Cornell, Penn, NC State (post-2020), USC, Ohio State, Texas A&M, Virginia Tech, U Minnesota–Twin Cities, UNC Chapel Hill, U Pittsburgh. Adding any of them requires finding a different primary source — see SOURCES.md for the dead-ends already explored.
