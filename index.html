Bachelor's, Master's, and PhD in CS

All three degree levels now included. Master's data comes from NCES IPEDS Digest Table 325.35, covering all U.S. degree-granting institutions in "Computer and Information Sciences" (CIP series 11). Bachelor's and PhD data come from the CRA Taulbee Survey for U.S. CS departments. Note the slight scope mismatch — covered explicitly in the methodology box.

⚠ Data source mismatch — please read

Bachelor's and PhD data: CRA Taulbee Survey, U.S. CS-PhD-granting departments only (~144 reporting departments). This is "Computer Science" specifically. Reports both total enrollment and new enrollment.

Master's data: NCES IPEDS Digest Table 325.35 covers all U.S. degree-granting institutions in "Computer and Information Sciences" (CIP series 11) — broader scope than CS-only. IPEDS reports degrees conferred, not enrollment. There is no clean national series for "new master's enrollment" that's directly comparable to Taulbee's "new BS / new PhD" numbers.

The result: master's data is on a different denominator (broader institutions, broader field, conferrals not enrollment). The chart shows it because it's the best master's series available, but the level shifts between master's and the others are not directly comparable. Year-over-year changes within each series remain meaningful.

What the panels show — read the numbers, not just the lines

Bachelor's: Both stock and flow climb steadily through GPT. New BS majors grew faster (+37% in 4 years) than total enrollment (+22%) — a wave that hasn't fully reached the upper-class enrollment yet.

Master's: CS-and-Information master's degrees conferred jumped 5% in 2020—21 (the COVID rebound), then dipped 5% in 2021—22, ending essentially flat through 2021—22. The 2022—23 IPEDS data is not yet in the Digest, but the Encoura/Eduventures analysis [S7] reports CIP 11.0701 (CS specifically) nearly doubled — from ~12,000 to ~22,000 conferrals — driven by international students returning post-COVID. The CS master's market is volatile and dominated by international demand.

PhD: Stock view shows smooth +8% growth. Flow view shows the COVID dip ('20—21: index 85.4), partial recovery, then a sharp +13.1% YoY jump in '23—24 — the first full academic year that began after ChatGPT. This is the clearest "GPT effect" signal in the verified data.

Per-university view · total enrollment · indexed

Pick a university. See how its CS pipeline shifted post-GPT.

Same idea as the national figures, but at one school at a time. Each value below was downloaded by scripts/fetch_university_data.py from the linked university institutional-research source — no third-party aggregators. Click any value's [src] to open the document it came from.

The y-axis shows an index: the school's earliest available year is set to 100, and every later year is scaled relative to that. The underlying measure is total enrollment headcount at the start of each Fall term — i.e. the count of students currently in the program (bachelor's, master's, or PhD), not degrees conferred and not first-time enrollment.

⚠ COVERAGE GAP — READ ME
Per-school enrollment data is hard to find as open primary sources. Most institutional-research offices publish degrees conferred (federally required for IPEDS) but not per-CS-major enrollment in a stable, parseable form. So this view only includes schools whose own IR/department pages publish multi-year CS enrollment headcounts: currently Georgia Tech (College of Computing fact book) and Michigan State (CSE Department page). The following schools were checked and excluded because their public sources do not publish enrollment in a usable form: CMU, UIUC, UMD, UC Berkeley. Adding any of them requires finding a different primary source — see SOURCES.md for the dead-ends already explored.

The ≈ RANK label on each pill is an approximate tier from CSRankings — specifically the AI-areas index (ai + vision + mlmining + nlp + inforet, 2014—2024 window). Schools are bucketed into ≈ rank 5 / 10 / 30 / 50, not given exact ranks; the ≈ glyph signals the imprecision. Tier assignment is hardcoded in scripts/fetch_university_data.py per school — it does not update automatically when CSRankings recomputes.
